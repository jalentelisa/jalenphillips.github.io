Webpage Redesign Concept

Role: UI/UX, Design Consultant
Team: Jalen Phillips (solo)


01. Overview

Kingdom Low Voltage Apprenticeship is a non-profit organization that offers young adults an opportunity to earn certifications in the lowvoltage industry. The organization is ran by Dedrick Torry who is a former sales person who scaled his career in order to mentor the youth. The goal of the organization is to increase enrollment by engaging in eye catching content that educates potential students and ultimately increase conversion rates.


02. Problem

The institution's website was outdated, difficult to navigate, and did not effectively highlight the various academic programs offered. As a result, prospective students struggled to find relevant information, leading to a decrease in engagement and a drop in enrollment applications. The website's design was also not aligned with the institution's branding, which contributed to a lack of visual appeal and consistency.


03. Design Process

To address these issues, I led a comprehensive website redesign focused on enhancing user experience, improving the visibility of academic programs, and rebranding the siteâ€™s color theory. This was followed by a comprehensive website audit and in-depth competitor research to pinpoint areas for improvement and identify opportunities for new features. Leveraging these insights, I developed low-fidelity wireframes that strategically positioned key elements to enhance the user experience. Once the layout was agreed, the mockups began.
The director loved the color green to demonstrate the idea of electricity as oppose to yellow. So, I integrated a cohesive and visually appealing color scheme that aligned with the institution's branding, contributing to a stronger and more consistent visual identity. I also implemented strategic calls-to-action that significantly boosted inquiry forms and enrollment applications. The redesign included optimized content and media presentation, better showcasing unique program features and leading to higher engagement rates across the site.
